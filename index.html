TeMuDance: Zero-Shot Textual Control for Music-Driven Dance Generation

Anonymous Authors
Code

Our model jointly conditions dances on music and text, producing sequences that are both rhythmically aligned and semantically controllable.

Abstract

Existing music-driven dance generation approaches have achieved strong realism and effective audio-motion alignment. However, they generally lack semantic controllability, making it difficult to guide specific movements through natural language descriptions. This limitation primarily stems from the absence of large-scale datasets that jointly align music, text, and motion for supervised learning of text-conditioned control. To address this challenge, we propose TeMuDance, a framework that enables text-based control for music-conditioned dance generation without requiring any manually annotated music–text–motion triplet dataset. TeMuDance introduces a motion-centred bridging paradigm that leverages motion as a shared semantic anchor to align disjoint music–dance and text–motion datasets within a unified embedding space, enabling cross-modal retrieval of missing modalities for end-to-end training. A lightweight text control branch is then trained on top of a frozen music-to-dance diffusion backbone, preserving rhythmic fidelity while enabling fine-grained semantic guidance. To further suppress noise inherent in the retrieved supervision, we design a dual-stream fine-tuning strategy with confidence-based filtering. We also propose a novel task-aligned metric that quantifies whether textual prompts induce the intended kinematic attributes under music conditioning.

Method

TeMuDance decouples training into two stages. We first pretrain a music-conditioned diffusion backbone that captures rhythm and style. Then, rather than collecting the paired music–text–motion triplets the field has been missing, we bridge disjoint music–dance and text–motion datasets through a shared motion-centred embedding space and retrieve the missing modality on the fly. A lightweight text control branch is fine-tuned on top of the frozen backbone, giving us fine-grained semantic steering without sacrificing rhythmic fidelity.

We learn a motion-centred bank by contrastively aligning disjoint text–motion and music–dance datasets in a shared motion space, enabling similarity-filtered modality completion to form pseudo triplets. A pretrained diffusion Transformer is frozen as the backbone, while a text control branch steers denoising and produces rhythm-aligned, semantically controllable dances.

Controllability at a Glance

Because the text control branch is attached to a frozen music backbone, TeMuDance supports three modes of generation from a single trained model. By adjusting the classifier-free guidance scales on the two conditioning pathways, the user can move smoothly between purely text-driven motion, purely music-driven dance, and joint text–music synthesis. The following sections walk through each of these modes in detail — starting from coarse genre-level style control, moving to fine-grained action-level prompts, and finishing with the unconditional music-only setting.

Genre Control

We begin with the coarsest level of textual control: the global stylistic character of the dance. During training, the text control branch sees composite instructions that concatenate action descriptions with coarse genre tags, so at inference time the user can steer the overall style through natural language. To isolate this effect, each set below fixes the input music and varies only the genre prompt, making the change attributable entirely to the textual instruction.

Music and Text Guided Generation

Moving from global style to fine-grained movement, we next show joint music and text conditioning at the action level.
